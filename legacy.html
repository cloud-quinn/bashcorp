Jabberwocky

Twas brillig and the slithy toves did gyre and gymbol in the wabe, all mimsy were the borogroves and the momeraths outgrabe. Beware the Jabberwock, my son! The jaws that bite… the claws that catch! Beware the Jubjub bird and shun the frumious Bandersnatch!

Part II

He took his vorpal sword in hand, long time the manxsome foe he soight. So rested he by the TumTum tree and stood awhile in thought. And, as in uffish thought he stood, the Jabberwock with eyes of flame came whiffling through the Tulgey Wood and burbled as it came.

Part III

One two! One two! And through and through! The vorpal blade went snicker-snack! He left it dead and, with its head, came galumphing back. And hast thou slain the Jabberwock? Come to my arms my beamish boy, oh frabjuous day! Callooh Callay! He chortled in his joy.
